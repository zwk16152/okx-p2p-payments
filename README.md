## **Escrow: The Reason P2P Payment Methods Are Safe to Use**

The biggest worry people have about P2P is "what if I send the money and the seller disappears with my crypto?" OKX handles this with an automated escrow.

Here's the mechanics:

1. When a buy order is placed, OKX locks the seller's crypto in an escrow account before you ever send fiat.
2. You send payment through your chosen payment method (bank transfer, PayPal, whatever you agreed on).
3. You tap **I have paid** inside the order — and you should screenshot your payment confirmation first.
4. The seller verifies the money arrived in their bank or wallet.
5. Only then does OKX release the crypto from escrow to your wallet.

If the seller stalls or claims they didn't receive payment when you have proof they did, you hit the **Appeal** button inside the order. OKX support steps in, reviews the screenshots and chat logs, and releases the funds accordingly. During any dispute, the crypto stays frozen — neither side can run off with it.

This is what makes it reasonable to plug your real bank account into a P2P marketplace: the crypto side is locked before your fiat side moves.

## **Avoiding Scams: What Actually Goes Wrong on P2P**

Escrow protects you from the obvious fraud, but P2P still has a handful of well-documented scam patterns you need to recognise. None of them are unique to OKX — they exist on every P2P platform — but they're worth naming explicitly.

**Fake payment receipts.** A buyer sends a doctored screenshot claiming they've paid, hoping the seller will release crypto before checking their actual bank account. Defence: as a seller, never release until you see the money in your account, not in a screenshot.

**Overpayment refund scams.** A buyer "accidentally" sends more than the order amount and asks you to refund the difference to a different account. Defence: never refund outside the order flow; if there's an overpayment, cancel and re-place the order through proper channels.

**Triangle scams.** A scammer acts as both buyer and seller in two separate trades, routing a third party's payment through you to launder funds. Defence: only trade with the counterparty in your order, never relay payments to anyone else.

**Off-platform communication lures.** Someone tries to move the conversation to Telegram, WhatsApp, or email to negotiate outside OKX's logged chat. Defence: never leave the in-app chat. If a dispute happens, OKX support can only review evidence from the platform's own chat logs.

**General hygiene rules worth memorising:**

- Prefer **Verified Merchants** — they've passed OKX's vetting and posted a security bond.
- Filter for counterparties with a **90%+ completion rate** and a meaningful trade history.
- Screenshot every payment confirmation before tapping **I have paid**.
- Only use payment methods registered in your own name.
- Enable two-factor authentication on your OKX account.
